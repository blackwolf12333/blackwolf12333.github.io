Quotes from Out of the software crisis

I've been reading Out of the Software Crisis by Baldur Bjarnason, and I thought I'd collect some quotes from the book that spoke to me here.

Skipping tests to save time is akin to setting a good portion of your budget on fire. Unit tests and types have a business purpose.

It’s popular today to believe that ‘good’ in books, film, music, or design is entirely subjective. It isn’t. Quality is relative, but it isn’t sub‐ jective.

It may sound ridiculous. But checklists (and similar abstractions) work. They are one of the most basic forms of system feedback available, but they absolutely do work. They save people’s lives before and after surgery. They prevent planes from crashing, and factories from exploding. They are good stuff
Skipping tests to save time is akin to setting a good portion of your budget on fire. Unit tests and types have a business purpose.
The business purpose of unit tests and types isn’t to prevent defects but to prevent variations in quality. Unexpected improvements in quality are just as undesirable as discovering a bug.
This suggests that we cannot maximize information generation by maximizing the success rate of a test. We maximize information con‐ tent of the test by approaching a 50 per cent failure rate. Our testing processes need to have an adequate failure rate to generate sufficient in‐ formation.
The most effective way to counteract the pop culture tendencies of both design and programming is research. This is a big reason why the programmers on a team should participate in the research process
